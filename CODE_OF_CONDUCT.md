# Code of Conduct

This Code of Conduct sets behavioural standards for participation in M0DUL0. It is designed to keep the community safe, legally sound, and a useful place to learn.

Participation in M0DUL0 means you agree to follow these rules.

This Code should be read together with [TERMS_OF_REFERENCE.md](TERMS_OF_REFERENCE.md), [CODE_OF_ETHICS.md](CODE_OF_ETHICS.md), and [DISCLAIMER.md](DISCLAIMER.md).

## 1) Community Standard

M0DUL0 is a hands-on technical community open to anyone, regardless of background. Members are expected to act with discipline, humility, and respect.

The culture is flat and low-ego. If you wrote something, your name is on it. If you don't know something, ask.

## 2) Scope of Acceptable Activity

Within M0DUL0, hardware research and related activity must be:

- Conducted on devices you own or have explicit permission to test.
- Lawful under Malaysian law, including the Computer Crimes Act 1997 [Act 563].
- For education, research, skill development, hardware modification, or CTF participation.
- Shared with the intent to teach and contribute to community resources, not to enable attacks on deployed systems.

If an activity is unclear or potentially harmful, stop and ask a moderator before proceeding.

## 3) Prohibited Conduct

The following are not allowed and may trigger immediate moderation action:

- Sharing PoCs, exploits, or techniques targeting devices or systems you don't own or aren't authorised to test.
- Doxxing, harassment, threats, intimidation, or targeted abuse of any kind.
- Posting firmware dumps, credentials, or device data obtained without the owner's consent.
- Malware development or distribution, even framed as educational.
- Using M0DUL0 as cover for commercial exploitation, undisclosed contract work, or unlawful activity.
- Transmitting on RF frequencies without the appropriate licence or class assignment under the Communications and Multimedia Act 1998 [Act 588].
- Requests for evasion tactics intended to bypass law-enforcement action or lawful platform controls.

## 4) Device and Data Handling

Members handling research artefacts must do so responsibly.

- Don't share full firmware dumps, keys, or credentials in community channels unless clearly necessary for the educational point and scrubbed of identifying data.
- Redact serial numbers, MAC addresses, and personal identifiers from screenshots and device captures unless directly relevant.
- Don't post data extracted from someone else's device without their consent, even if obtained incidentally during research.

## 5) Reporting a Concern

If you see a possible breach:

1. Preserve evidence with context, timestamp, and channel location.
2. Report privately to a Volunteer or Core Founding Team member.
3. Do not escalate in public channels.

False, malicious, or retaliatory reports may also be moderated.

## 6) Moderation and Enforcement

M0DUL0 applies a graduated warning model while retaining discretion based on risk severity.

- First breach: written warning and corrective guidance.
- Repeated or serious breach: temporary mute, access restriction, or removal pending review.
- Severe, harmful, or unlawful breach: immediate removal.

Volunteers and Core Founding Team members may act without prior warning where urgency, safety, or legal sensitivity requires it.

## 7) Decision Basis and Discretion

Decisions are made in good faith using available evidence, context, and community risk. Not every case fits a checklist. Volunteer and Core Founding Team judgement is used to keep the community safe, lawful, and operationally useful.

## 8) Final Note

This Code sets community rules, not legal advice. Each member is responsible for their own conduct and legal compliance.

For ethical practice standards, refer to [CODE_OF_ETHICS.md](CODE_OF_ETHICS.md).
