# Community Engagement

M0DUL0 uses a structured engagement model to build practical hardware security skills and keep the community active and learning together.

## Current Activities

### Weekly CTFs

- **Focus:** competitive hacking challenges across all formats — Traditional Jeopardy, OSINT, Hardware/IoT, AI, and others.
- **Format:** team participation in publicly available CTF competitions on a weekly cadence where possible.
- **Expected Output:** post-competition writeups submitted to M0DUL0's GitHub repositories with full credit attached.
- **Learning Goal:** build practical skills across all categories and strengthen M0DUL0's visibility and credibility as a community.

CTF announcements and team coordination happen in Discord.

**Contributing Writeups**

Members not yet in the M0DUL0 GitHub organisation should fork the relevant repository, commit their writeup to their fork, and open a pull request against `main`. See [CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md) for the full submission standard and checklist.

We encourage all members to join the M0DUL0 GitHub organisation directly — reach out to a Volunteer or Core Founding Team member on Discord to get an invite. Organisation members can push to branches and contribute without the fork step.

## Planned Activities

### Research Sessions

Long-term hardware security research sessions built around hands-on work with proper lab equipment. Research scope, datasets, and tooling are maintained in the [HARDWARE](https://github.com/[ORG]/HARDWARE) repository.

Research sessions are not scheduled for 2025. They will be announced through Discord when the community is ready to run them.

### Live Community Workshops and Meetups

In-person hardware hacking workshops and community meetups, each centred around a specific theme or topic. Planned workshop tracks include:

- Beginner Fundamentals
- Soldering and Rework
- Logic Analysis
- Firmware and Chip-Off Extraction
- and more to be announced.

No dates are confirmed yet. Updates will be posted in Discord and on LinkedIn when planning begins.

## Participation Standard

- Contribute respectfully and credit sources and prior work where applicable.
- Ask technical questions with enough context for others to help effectively.
- Prioritise quality of analysis and documentation over speed.
