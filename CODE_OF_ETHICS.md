# Code of Ethics

## M0DUL0 SEC

**Effective Date:** [fill in]
**Applies To:** All M0DUL0 members, contributors, and participants across all channels, repositories, events, and activities.

## 1) Core Philosophy

Hardware hacking sits close to real-world systems: devices in homes, vehicles, hospitals, and industrial facilities. That proximity demands responsibility. M0DUL0 is open to anyone, regardless of background, and treats hardware security as a tool for education, research, and community knowledge-building. It is not for exploitation, ego, or personal gain.

## 2) Principle 1: Legality and Accountability

M0DUL0 members shall:

- Conduct all research in compliance with Malaysian law, including the Computer Crimes Act 1997 [Act 563], Communications and Multimedia Act 1998 [Act 588], Copyright Act 1987 [Act 332], and Personal Data Protection Act 2010 [Act 709] including the 2024 Amendment where applicable.
- Only test, modify, or extract data from devices they own or have explicit, documented permission to touch.
- Recognise that M0DUL0 does not authorise research against third-party hardware, networks, or production systems. Each member is personally accountable for their own actions.

## 3) Principle 2: Responsible Disclosure

M0DUL0 members who discover vulnerabilities in vendor products shall:

- Notify the affected vendor or maintainer privately before publishing.
- Allow reasonable time for a fix before releasing full technical details.
- Publish enough detail for the community to learn from, without enabling attacks on unpatched, deployed devices.
- Not sell vulnerabilities, exploits, or tools to parties with malicious intent.

Refer to OWASP's [Vulnerability Disclosure Cheat Sheet](https://cheatsheetseries.owasp.org/cheatsheets/Vulnerability_Disclosure_Cheat_Sheet.html) for the full disclosure playbook.

## 4) Principle 3: Integrity and Reproducibility

M0DUL0 members shall:

- Document research with enough detail for others to reproduce and verify.
- Distinguish clearly between confirmed findings, working hypotheses, and speculation.
- Credit prior work accurately and completely in writeups and references.
- Not misrepresent the scope, impact, or novelty of their research.

## 5) Principle 4: Privacy and Minimisation

M0DUL0 adopts an ethics of minimisation.

Members shall:

- Collect only the data necessary for the stated educational or research objective.
- Redact personal identifiers, credentials, and serial numbers from shared artefacts unless directly relevant to the research point.
- Not use M0DUL0 channels to circulate personal data found incidentally on a research device.

## 6) Principle 5: Professionalism

M0DUL0 members shall:

- Conduct themselves in ways that reflect positively on the community and the hardware security field.
- Maintain respectful, low-ego communication on Discord, GitHub, LinkedIn, and at events.
- Share knowledge for the benefit of the community, not to posture or gatekeep.
- Prioritise constructive learning and community safety over offensive exploitation.

## 7) Ethical Boundaries in Practice

The following conduct is inconsistent with this Code:

- Testing, modifying, or extracting data from devices without ownership or explicit permission.
- Sharing working exploits against unpatched, deployed production devices.
- Harassment, intimidation, or targeted abuse of any kind.
- Representing personal research as community-endorsed findings without authorisation from moderators.

Where ethical risk is unclear, pause and ask a Volunteer or Core Founding Team member before proceeding.

## 9) Review

Moderators may update this document as M0DUL0 grows, as legal context changes, or as the community's research scope evolves. Updates will be communicated through Discord.

## 10) Final Commitment

By participating in M0DUL0, members affirm a commitment to lawful conduct, responsible disclosure, research integrity, and a community-first approach to hardware security.
