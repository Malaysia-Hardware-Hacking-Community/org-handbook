## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Terms of Reference sets out the organisational model, role accountabilities, operational procedures, and governance boundaries of M0DUL0.

M0DUL0 is an independent hardware hacking community based in Malaysia. Its purpose is to make hardware security accessible to anyone, through structured resources, materials, research papers, and hands-on projects, organised around traditional CTF categories as a learning framework.

## 2. Core Principles

- Legality and consent first.
- Ethics by default.
- Responsible disclosure over public dumps.
- Reproducible writeups and source integrity.
- Low-ego, credit-where-it's-due collaboration.

## 3. Definitions

### 3.1 Hardware Hacking

**Hardware hacking** means the lawful study, modification, and security testing of physical computing devices and embedded systems, including firmware, protocols, and electronics, on devices the practitioner owns or has explicit permission to test.

### 3.2 Proof-of-Concept (PoC)

A **PoC** is a working demonstration of a vulnerability or technique, built to prove and document a finding rather than to cause harm or enable attacks on deployed, unpatched devices.

### 3.3 Responsible Disclosure

**Responsible disclosure** means privately reporting a vulnerability to the affected vendor or maintainer and giving them reasonable time to fix it before publishing full technical details.

## 4. Organisational Structure

M0DUL0 currently operates a four-tier hierarchy:

1. **Advisors**
2. **Core Founding Team**
3. **Volunteers**
4. **Members**

This is M0DUL0's working structure as an independent community. As the community matures, M0DUL0 intends to formalise as a registered society under the **Societies Act 1966 [Act 335]** with the Registry of Societies (ROS) Malaysia, at which point the hierarchy will transition to the statutory committee structure required under that Act. Governance documents will be updated accordingly at that stage.

## 5. Job Scope

### Advisors

- Provide strategic guidance and oversight to the Core Founding Team.
- Contribute expertise, networks, and external perspective to support M0DUL0's direction and growth.
- Not involved in day-to-day operations.

### Core Founding Team

- Founders and co-founders collectively responsible for all aspects of M0DUL0's operation, including operations, management, treasury, marketing, and administration.
- Make final decisions on community governance, partnerships, and resource allocation.
- Set the technical direction of the community and oversee research, events, and content output.

### Volunteers

- Frontline community support and enforcement.
- Moderate Discord channels and enforce the Code of Conduct as the first point of contact for member concerns and reports.
- Support research sessions, events, and other community activities as directed by the Core Founding Team.
- Appointed by the Core Founding Team based on conduct, reliability, and community standing.

### Members

- The community itself: students, professionals, hobbyists, researchers, and anyone with an interest in hardware security.
- Participate in research sessions, CTF competitions, hardware projects, and community discussions.
- Contribute writeups, resources, and projects to the community's knowledge base.
- Expected to follow the Code of Conduct and Code of Ethics at all times.

## 6. Legal and Ethical Framework

Malaysia Hardware Hacking Community (MHHC) is strictly a learning community for education, ethical research, and lawful skill development. M0DUL0 is not an operational tasking body and does not authorise testing of devices, networks, or systems without the owner's permission.

Members remain individually responsible for complying with relevant Malaysian law, including:

- **Computer Crimes Act 1997 [Act 563]**, on unauthorised access to or modification of computer material, which extends to firmware and embedded systems.
- **Communications and Multimedia Act 1998 [Act 588]**, which governs radio spectrum use. RF/SDR work that transmits, not just receives, may require a licence or fall under a class assignment.
- **Copyright Act 1987 [Act 332]**, relevant to firmware reverse engineering and decompilation.
- **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 [Act 709]**, including the 2024 Amendment, where research involves personal data recovered from a device.

All members remain individually liable for their own research decisions and legal compliance.

## 7. Operational Procedures

### 7.1 Joining and Onboarding

New members join through Discord via the community Linktree. Discord is M0DUL0's main platform. Onboarding is handled directly in the server through Discord's built-in onboarding flow, where new members self-assign their roles before gaining access to community channels.

Members select their experience level and one or more fields of interest:

- **CTFs**: focused on CTF competitions and challenge-based learning.
- **Researcher**: focused on hardware security research, PoCs, and writeups.
- **Modifier**: focused on hardware modification and hands-on hardware projects.

Members can select any combination. Once roles are assigned, full access to the community is granted automatically by the server.

### 7.2 Discipline

Discipline is enforced under the Code of Conduct using a graduated model:

- **First breach:** written warning and corrective guidance.
- **Repeated or serious breach:** temporary mute or access restriction.
- **Severe, harmful, or unlawful breach:** immediate removal.

Moderators may act without prior warning where safety or legal sensitivity requires it.

### 7.3 Volunteerism and Merit

M0DUL0 roles are volunteer-driven. Role continuity is based on conduct, reliability, and contribution quality.
